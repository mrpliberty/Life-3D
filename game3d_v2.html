3D Conway Rules

Grid: 10 × 10 × 10 = 1,000 cells

Neighbors: 26 per cell (3×3×3 cube − center)

Topology: Toroidal (wraparound on all faces)

Birth: Dead cell with exactly 3 live neighbors → alive

Survive: Live cell with 2, 3, 4, or 5 live neighbors → stays alive

Die: All other cases

Why 3D?

• More neighbors (26 vs 8) changes balance

• Patterns move in 3 spatial dimensions

• Higher interaction density → different emergent behavior

Complexity: O(n³) per generation

• 10³ = 1K cells × 26 neighbors = 26K checks/gen
